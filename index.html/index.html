Biography

Subhas Chandra Bose (23 January 1897 – 18 August 1945) was an Indian nationalist leader who fought for India’s independence. He reorganized the Indian National Army (INA), and was known popularly as “Netaji.”

Born in Cuttack, Bengal Presidency, Bose studied in Calcutta and England, but gave up a civil service career to join the freedom movement. He believed in active struggle and was a charismatic leader. His slogans like “Jai Hind” and “Chalo Delhi” inspired many.

Legacy & Impact

Bose’s work left an enduring imprint on India’s freedom movement. The Indian National Army (INA) army he revived became a symbol of sacrifice and patriotism. His slogan “Jai Hind” is still used widely in India. Many memorials, museums, and institutions are dedicated to him across India.

The mystery surrounding his death in 1945 continues to intrigue historians and the public alike. Whether he died in a plane crash in Taiwan or survived is still debated.
